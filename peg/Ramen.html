Miso Ramen Recipe

Miso ramen is a delicious and savory Japanese noodle soup that combines the rich flavors of miso paste with a variety of toppings. Here's how you can make it at home:

Ingredients

  • 5 tablespoons miso paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 Â½ cm piece of ginger, grated
  • 12 shiitake mushrooms
  • 225 grams smoked tofu, cut into 4 slices
  • 2 tablespoons tamari (or liquid aminos)
  • 250 grams soba noodles
  • 16 ears baby corn
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 8 baby pak choi
  • 200 grams ready-to-eat beansprouts
  • 2 red chillies, finely sliced
  • 2 spring onions, finely sliced
  • 4 tablespoons crispy seaweed
  • 2 tablespoons black sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (to finish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and cook the soba noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a separate pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the grated ginger and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add the miso paste, soy sauce, and tamari to the pot, stirring to combine.
  4. Pour in enough water to create a broth, then add the shiitake mushrooms and baby corn. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  5. In a frying pan, cook the smoked tofu slices until they are golden brown on both sides.
  6. Divide the cooked soba noodles among four bowls. Pour the miso broth over the noodles.
  7. Top each bowl with the smoked tofu, baby pak choi, beansprouts, red chillies, spring onions, crispy seaweed, and black sesame seeds.
  8. Drizzle with sesame oil to finish. Serve hot and enjoy!
