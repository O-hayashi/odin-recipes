Sashimi Recipe

Sashimi is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of fresh, thinly sliced raw fish or seafood. It's often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger. Here's how you can prepare it at home:

Ingredients

  • 4 oz (110 g) fresh salmon
  • 4 oz (110 g) fresh tuna
  • 4 oz (110 g) fresh yellowtail
  • 1 daikon radish, julienned
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 4 shiso leaves (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon wasabi
  • Pickled ginger (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Ensure the fish is sushi-grade and extremely fresh. Ask your fishmonger to cut it into sashimi blocks if possible.
  2. Using a sharp knife, slice the fish into thin, bite-sized pieces.
  3. Arrange the fish slices on a serving plate, along with the julienned daikon radish, cucumber, and carrot.
  4. Place the shiso leaves on the plate if using.
  5. In a small bowl, mix the sesame oil and soy sauce.
  6. Serve the sashimi with the sesame-soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger on the side.
