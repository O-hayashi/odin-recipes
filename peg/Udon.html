Udon Recipe

Udon is a type of thick, chewy Japanese noodle made from wheat flour. It's often served in a simple broth or stir-fried with various ingredients. Here's how you can make a basic udon noodle soup at home:

Ingredients

  • 2 packets udon noodles (fresh or frozen)
  • 4 cups dashi broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (shiitake or your choice)
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 soft-boiled egg (optional)
  • 1 sheet nori (seaweed), cut into strips
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the udon noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, bring the dashi broth to a boil. Add the soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and salt. Stir to combine.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the broth and simmer for 5 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender.
  4. Divide the cooked udon noodles among serving bowls. Pour the hot broth and mushrooms over the noodles.
  5. Top each bowl with baby spinach, green onions, a soft-boiled egg (if using), nori strips, and sesame seeds.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious udon noodle soup!
